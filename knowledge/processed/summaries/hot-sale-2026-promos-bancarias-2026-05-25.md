## Best Observed Fits

- Best high-ticket upside: Banorte contado can be strongest if the purchase fits its merchant list and digital-card requirements. The page reports up to $12,000 bonus for purchases above $45,000, and intermediate tiers can produce high effective returns near their lower bounds.
- Best MSI stack candidate: Santander has 15% base plus daily additional 10%, or 15% with payroll, but the daily merchant calendar matters and the cap is $8,000.
- Best simple MSI candidates: HSBC Premier/One can reach 20% or 30% under narrower customer conditions; BBVA and Banamex are simpler 15% style offers but have lower caps or longer MSI requirements.
- Best fixed-value Amex fit: American Express can be attractive around exact thresholds, especially $1,500 on $7,500 or $2,000 on $10,000 where eligible.
- Best Amazon coupon overlays observed: Amazon listed Mifel and Scotiabank at 20%, Invex at 15%, and several major banks at 10%, but these must be checked against bank-wide promo stacking rules.
- Low-value or lottery-like promos: Nu, DiDi daily top-user bonus, and Banamex Horas Banamex depend heavily on chance, timing, limited redemptions, or non-guaranteed availability.

## Practical Ranking Heuristic

1. First choose the store and basket total; many offers are merchant-specific and non-cumulative.
2. Reject any promo that requires a bank/card/customer segment the buyer does not have.
3. Prefer guaranteed statement bonuses over lottery, first-users, limited canjes, or points unless the points value is known.
4. Compare effective benefit after cap: benefit / required spend at the actual basket amount.
5. Verify registration and excluded payment rails before paying, especially PayPal, Mercado Pago, digital wallets, and Amazon seller/shipping restrictions.

## Risk Flags

- Several benefits require registration or activation before purchase.
- Some promotions only apply to digital cards.
- Wallet rails are frequently excluded by bank promos even when store coupons mention wallets.
- Caps differ by day, client, card type, transaction, or promotion; do not assume a single cap rule.
- Store coupons may not stack with bank statement credits.
